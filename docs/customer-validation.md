# SiteAgentAI — Customer Validation Guide

A practical playbook for showing the MVP to small service business owners and testing whether they would pay for it.

---

## 1. One-sentence pitch

**SiteAgentAI is an AI website employee that captures visitor requests on your site and delivers you ready-to-act leads with an AI summary and recommended follow-up.**

---

## 2. Two-minute demo script

Use this flow when sitting with a business owner (in person, on a call, or sharing your screen).

### Minute 1 — Show the visitor experience

1. Open your live demo homepage.
2. Say: *"Imagine this is your website. A visitor lands here after hours or while your team is busy."*
3. Click **Talk to AI Employee** in the bottom-right corner.
4. Walk through the form briefly: name, email, phone, service needed, urgency, message.
5. Submit a test lead that matches their business (e.g. "AC not cooling" for HVAC).
6. Show the thank-you screen and say: *"The visitor knows their request was received. You get their contact details plus an AI summary."*

### Minute 2 — Show the owner dashboard

1. Open **/admin** (owner dashboard preview).
2. Point to a sample lead card and walk through:
   - Customer name, email, phone
   - Service needed and urgency
   - **AI Summary** — what the customer wants, in plain language
   - **Recommended next action** — what to do next and when
3. Close with: *"Instead of a vague contact form email, you open your dashboard and know exactly who to call and what to say."*
4. Scroll to **Early Pilot Offer** and say: *"We're opening a small number of spots at $49/month for early pilots."*

**Tip:** Let them talk more than you do. Ask *"Would this help you?"* before pitching price.

## 3. Questions to ask business owners

Ask these after the demo, not before. Listen for pain, not politeness.

### Pain and current process

- How do website visitors contact you today?
- Do you ever miss leads because no one responded fast enough?
- What happens when someone fills out your contact form at night or on weekends?
- How long does it usually take someone on your team to follow up?

### Value and willingness to pay

- Would a summary like this save you time on every new inquiry?
- Is knowing the urgency upfront useful for your team?
- If this worked on your real website, would $49/month feel reasonable?
- What would it need to do for you to say yes today?

### Fit check

- What type of jobs do most website leads turn into for you?
- Who on your team would use this dashboard daily?
- What tools do you use now (CRM, email, phone, spreadsheets)?

## 5. What counts as a strong validation signal

These are green lights — prioritize follow-up within 24 hours.

- **They ask about timeline:** *"When can this go on my site?"*
- **They name a specific pain:** missed calls, slow follow-up, bad form submissions, after-hours leads lost
- **They ask about price unprompted** or say $49/month sounds fair or cheap
- **They want to show a partner, office manager, or spouse** — means they're seriously considering it
- **They offer intros** to other business owners who have the same problem
- **They compare it favorably** to hiring someone or missing leads
- **They say yes to a pilot** or ask what onboarding looks like

**Weak signal (don't count as validation yet):**

- *"Looks cool"* or *"Nice demo"* with no follow-up questions
- *"Send me info"* with no pain articulated
- They can't describe how leads come in today

## 6. What objections to listen for

Don't argue. Write these down — they shape the product.

| Objection | What it usually means | How to respond |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------|
| *"I already get enough leads."* | No perceived pain | Ask: *"Do you ever lose leads because response time is slow?"* |
| *"My customers call, they don't use forms."* | Channel mismatch | Ask: *"What about after hours or when the office is busy?"* |
| *"I don't trust AI."* | Trust gap | Emphasize: they still get contact details; AI just summarizes the request |
| *"Too expensive."* | Price/value mismatch | Ask: *"What is one missed job worth to you?"* |
| *"I need to think about it."* | Normal — not a no | Ask: *"What would you need to see to feel confident?"* |
| *"Does it integrate with X?"* | Real buyer question | Note the tool; say integrations are on the roadmap for pilots |
| *"Will it work on my website?"* | Implementation concern | Reassure: early pilot includes setup help |

## 7. Next steps after someone says yes

When a business owner agrees to the early pilot:

1. **Capture basics immediately** (on the call or right after):
   - Business name
   - Owner name and best contact (phone + email)
   - Service type (HVAC, plumbing, etc.)
   - Website URL
   - How they get leads today

2. **Send a short confirmation email** within 24 hours:
   - Thank them for joining the early pilot
   - Recap: $49/month, what's included
   - Next step: you'll schedule a 15-minute onboarding call
   - Ask them to reply with their preferred contact method

3. **Schedule onboarding** (15–30 min):
   - Walk through the demo again on their terms
   - Learn their most common customer requests
   - Set expectation: pilot uses demo data until real backend is live

4. **Track in a simple list** (spreadsheet is fine for now):

   | Business | Contact | Service type | Said yes date | Status | Notes |
   |----------|---------|--------------|---------------|--------|-------|

5. **Follow up weekly** until they're live or they drop out:
   - Week 1: onboarding call
   - Week 2: check if they've shared the demo with their team
   - Week 3: ask for one referral if they're happy

6. **Ask for validation proof** after 2–4 weeks:
   - Would they pay $49/month when billing starts?
   - Would they recommend it to another owner?
   - One sentence testimonial (even informal text is gold)
