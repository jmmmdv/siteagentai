# SiteAgentAI — Validation Tracker

Use this spreadsheet-style tracker during customer discovery. Copy the table into Notion, Google Sheets, or keep it here and add rows as you go.

**Early pilot price:** $49/month  
**Weekly goal:** Contact 5 businesses

---

## How to use this tracker

1. **Add a row before you reach out** — fill in business name, industry, contact person, and contact method.
2. **Log the date contacted** when you send the first message or have the first conversation.
3. **Mark demo shown** after they see the live demo (yours or theirs on a call).
4. **Capture reaction in their words** — short quotes beat your interpretation (e.g. *"We lose leads after hours"*).
5. **Record willingness to pay** — Yes / No / Maybe based on what they said, not what you hope.
6. **Note the main objection** — even if they said yes (price, trust, timing, etc.).
7. **Set a follow-up date** — usually 3–5 days after first contact, or next day after a demo.
8. **Write one next action** — one concrete step (e.g. *"Send demo link"*, *"Schedule 10-min call"*, *"Close loop — not a fit"*).
9. **Review every Friday** — count strong vs. weak signals and apply the decision rule below.

**Tip:** One row per business. If you talk to the same owner twice, update the row — don't create duplicates.

## Strong validation signal

A **strong signal** means the owner sees real value — not just politeness.

Count as **strong** if they say or do any of the following:

- **Yes** or **strong Maybe** to paying $49/month
- They ask **when it can go live** on their website
- They name a **specific pain** (missed leads, slow follow-up, bad form submissions, after-hours gaps)
- They want to **show a partner, office manager, or spouse**
- They **offer an intro** to another business owner
- They ask **practical buyer questions** (setup, timeline, who uses the dashboard)
- They compare it favorably to **missing jobs** or **hiring help**

**Strong Maybe** = clear interest + one blocker you can address (timing, need to see real site, want a call). Log the blocker in **Main objection**.

---

## Weak validation signal

A **weak signal** is friendly but doesn't validate willingness to pay.

Count as **weak** if:

- *"Looks cool"* or *"Nice demo"* with no follow-up questions
- *"Send me info"* with no pain described
- They can't explain **how website leads work today**
- They ghost after demo with no reply (log as **No** until they re-engage)
- *"Maybe someday"* with no timeline or next step
- They're not the decision-maker and won't connect you to one

**Don't count weak signals toward the decision rule.** They're learning, not validation.

**Contact 5 businesses per week.**

| Metric | Target |

## Decision rule

After each batch of **5 contacted businesses** (with at least 3 demos shown when possible), apply this rule:

### Continue building

**If 3 out of 5 say Yes or strong Maybe** to $49/month (or equivalent strong signal):

- Keep building toward real lead storage, notifications, and pilot onboarding
- Prioritize what blockers repeat in **Main objection**
- Convert strong Maybes with follow-up calls, not new features

### Improve positioning first

**If 0 out of 5 show interest** (all No or weak signals):

- **Do not add major features yet**
- Revisit pitch, homepage hero, and demo flow
- Test new positioning with the next 5 businesses
- Read objections column — if the same objection appears 3+ times, fix messaging before code

### Mixed results (1–2 interested)

- Run **one more batch of 5** before big product decisions
- Double down on industries or owners who reacted strongly
- Ignore one-off polite nos
